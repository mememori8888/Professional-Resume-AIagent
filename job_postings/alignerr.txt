What You'll Do
Submit two real artifacts from the same project: one requirements document and one final design document
Ensure both artifacts are genuine deliverables from actual engineering work, not written or recreated for this submission
Redact identifying information (company names, customer names, personnel, proprietary part numbers) if needed, and note any redactions in the metadata
Package both artifacts into a single .zip file with the required folder structure
Populate all required metadata fields accurately
Confirm you have the legal right to share the material
Who You Are
Practicing or former hardware or systems engineer with real project experience
Your discipline is one of the following: mechanical, aerospace, electrical, materials, chemical, civil, controls, manufacturing, or systems engineering, software-only engineering does not qualify
You have existing project artifacts you are legally allowed to share, no active NDA blocking, no ITAR or EAR controlled content
Strong written English skills for metadata and submission accuracy
What Makes a Perfect Submission
A requirements document that was actually used to drive the design, including functional requirements, performance thresholds, interface requirements, constraints, and assumptions
A final design document that represents the real deliverable: CAD files, schematics, stress analyses, drawing packages, simulation outputs, or equivalent
Both artifacts are from the same project and clearly traceable to each other
Tradeoffs, waived requirements, or scope changes are included if they exist in the original documentation
What Does Not Qualify
Documents created or rewritten specifically for this submission
Coursework, textbook examples, or fabricated case studies
Software-only projects
Marketing materials, sales decks, or executive summaries presented as design documents
Submissions where you do not have the legal right to share the artifacts
ITAR or EAR export-controlled documents
